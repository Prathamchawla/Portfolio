Tesla Stock Price Prediction

About the Data

We are doing Univariate Analysis on the Close Prices of the Tesla Stock.

EDA of the Data

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is a crucial step in understanding the characteristics of a dataset. In the context of Tesla stock, conducting EDA can help identify trends, patterns, and outliers in the historical close prices.



1. Line Charts: A line chart is a powerful tool for visualizing the trend in Tesla's closing stock prices over time.



2. Histogram: A histogram helps visualize the distribution of Tesla's closing prices, providing insights into the frequency of different price ranges.





3. Box Plot: A box plot provides a summary of the central tendency, dispersion, and identification of outliers in the closing prices.

Smoothing of the Data

Smoothing of data involves reducing noise or fluctuations in a dataset to reveal underlying patterns or trends more clearly. In the context of financial data, such as Tesla stock prices, smoothing techniques can help analysts identify the overall direction of the market, filter out short-term fluctuations, and make it easier to spot long-term trends.

Seasonal Decompose

Seasonal decomposition of time series (STL) is a technique that decomposes a time series into three main components: trend, seasonality, and remainder (residuals). This technique is useful for understanding the underlying patterns in the data and can be applied to financial time series, such as Tesla stock prices.

Forecasting on the Test Data

To forecast the test data and plot the results, you can use various time series forecasting methods. One common method is the AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average (ARIMA) model.
